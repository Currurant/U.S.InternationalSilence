Q: So they are far away from home and paying more money to attend school. Then what are their motivations? What attracts them to U.S.?

A: According to "Chinese Students? Motivations for Studying in the United States" published on Journal of International Students, there are three reasons that drive Chinese students to U.S.(Chao, Hegarty, Angelidis, &Lu, 2017):

1. Able to attend better school in U.S. than in China, due to the fierce competition in College Entry Exam.

2. More majors and fields are offered in U.S. 

3. The living environment is better, such as less people, cleaner air, etc.

Now that you know some details about international students, it is time to build up some sympathy for them.

What are they sufferring?

In general, Acculturation Depression is what all international students can potentially fall prey to. It refers to the psychological impact on a person when he/she is going through adaptation to a new culture. Contrary to what many students do, this effect will not be mitigated by self-effacy or self-concealment, which are both correlated with depression even among Americans (Constantine, Okazaki, & Utsey, 2004).  In other words, being socially inactive (silent) would exacerbate the situation. There are a few reasons why they are inactive in U.S. colleges:

1. Language Barriers

Study found that English fluency is negatively associated with acculturation anxiety and depression (Constantine, Okazaki, & Utsey, 2004). In particular, language is more of an issue for Asian students than European ones (Fritz, Chin, & DeMarinis, 2008).

2. Hard Time Being Apart from Families

Based on a survey and ANOVA conducted by Fritz, Chin and DeMarinis (2008), comparing to Asian counterpart, European students are having harder time from being apart from their families.

3. Financial Difficulties

International students pay about twice as much tuition as residents. (This is true only for public schools.)

Since being inactive will worsen the existing anxiety or depression, the key to resolve this problem is to activate international students. Before we propose any strategies, let's take a look at our International Silence survey and see how serious or common this is among the people near us.
