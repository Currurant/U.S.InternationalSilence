Limitations of this survey

1. The sample size is only 26--not large enough to complement more complicated statistical analysis. Hopefully, more people can take it in the future, following the link down below.

2. The sample might also be biased and underestimating the seriousness and urgency of the problem--most of the participants are friends of ours and as far as we know, they do not have severe anxiety from acculturation in U.S.

3. For those who are inactive, we did not ask them the underlying reasons--which can be hard to answer even by themselves, since some behaviors are unconscious or natural, given their background culture.

For students

Taking part in activities in schools, no matter it is inside or outside the classroom can help foreigners feel more comfortable and adopt to the environment faster. Here we give personal tips for students who are reluctant or unable to do this, due to the first four factors found by our survey.

Personality

If you are a private or insecure person and constantly feel overwhelmed by social networking, try reading English articles, listening to podcast or watching live shows or TV series. Also, try making a few close friends instead of many fair-weather friends--since you are not comfortable with being surrounded by many people anyway.

Gender

For guys who are all but addicted to video games, please spend more time outside or at least in the real world. It is true that video games these days are intriguing and can teach a lot, finding the balance between virtual world and reality is as fulfilling and memorable as an "Aced".

Academic Level

For seniors and graduate students, despite that job hunting and research duties are overwhelming and frustrating, spending a decent amount of time on student organizations can refresh your mind and produce new spark of enlightenment. Plus, there is a reason why you guys have a higher depression rate than average.

English Proficiency

English might be the biggest barrier for you all, but shying away from speaking or keep self-concealment is the start of a vicious circle. Therefore, don't be shy, serize your chance, take your time and speak up--if you think your English can't be any worse, why not try something new!

For instructors and educators

Since our survey reflects that most students view instructors' personality and approach as a critical factor for them to be active, we did some research and based on what we found, here are our suggestions.

1. Instructors have to realize that international students might have very different expectations for their teachers and peers. Thanks to Wang (2016), we know that Chinese students tend to view peer review as "teaching other people" or an activity of "problem-identification" and "error-correction" instead of a process of co-construction through communication and negotiation. 

Tips: Instructors who are aware of this problem should make his/her students familiar with the concept of peer review and the expectation of it. Also, providing peer review guidelines can demonstrate to the students how it should be done and thus eliminates the misunderstanding.

2. Study shows that even when international students are seemingly active in class, the questions they ask are clarification ones and their answers are often short and only in a few words (Yildirim, 2017)

Tips: Instructors who notice this can ask followup questions like "Why do you think this is?" or "Is there another possibility?" Try to get more quality answers from international students and don't settle for easy, simple answers.

3. Even though it is usually the case that international students do not spontaneously start a discussion or try to explain things in class, it does not mean that they cannot contribute to the fund of knowledge. Credit to Ozgur Yildirim (2017)--who conducted a series of comparisons between international students and domestic ones--we know that international students are likely to contribute when they are assigned the questions in advance. The reason behind it is mainly language issue--they just cannot organize and express their thoughts as fast as domestic students.

Tips: Instructors can assign some questions directly to some students before classes, which will give them the time to organzie their opinions and find the proper expressions to use.
