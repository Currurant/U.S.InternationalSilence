Activities are critical for internationals students to get involved in and adopt to the new cultural environment. Lack of language proficiency is the No.1 reason why internationals students are less active than Americans (Yildirim, 2017). Our survey aims to evaluate the activity level of international students, both in and outside the classroom.

All the explanations below are based on this Survey Results Analysis. (As limited size as our data is, there are some notable partterns.)

Below are some basic information of our participants:

Chinese students take up majority of our participants, which is understandable given the distribution of international students in U.S.

Based on the schools our participants are attending, we can expect that the overall degree of silence in U.S. is worse than our results--these are all top schools in U.S.

Our participants are with all academic levels except for sophomore

Most participants are studying in the fields with large job markets, such as electrical engineering, statistics and computer science.

Fortuitously, our participants split in half for each gender.

Below are some analysis based on the results we have gotten:

We assigned activity level to each participants, according to their activity scores--which are evaluated based on the following:

There are three questions that inquire about activity level in the classroom, club or organization and during a group project. For each question that asks about activity level, a participant will score the following points with corresponding answer:

3 points: active and forthcoming
2 points: participating when led by others
1 point: quiet and reticent

We then sum up the points for each question and obtain an overall activity score, based on which an overall activity level is assigned:

7 points or greater: active (A person has to be active in at least one category (score 3 points) to be considered overall active.)

4 points or less: inactive (A person who is inactive in at least two categories will be considered overall inactive.)

inbetween: reserved

We analyze each potential factor that can influence the activity level:

Personality

From the personality-activity level contigency table, we can tell that personality is one of the influential factors for activity level--since the only two inactive participants have private personality.

Gender

It appears that girls are more active than guys--there is no inactive girls. However, in guys' defense, we are pretty active in E-sports.

Academic Level

Higher standing participants are less active than lower ones, which can be explained by heavier coursework, job searching or research responsibilities.

English Proficiency

The results coincide with our expectation and former research results (English proficiency is negatively associated with acculturation.) The only two inactive participants show up in the lowest proficiency level, while participants with highest proficiency (at least they think they are) are more likely to be active (50% of them are and there is no inactive)

Instructor and Curriculum

When a person do not care about the personality of the instructor or the relevancy of the course, he/she is likely to be inactive. It should be noted that most participants (19/26) consider personality of the instructor critical for their activity level in class.

Being active in class and extracurricular events helps a person adopt to new environment (acculturation), thus pacifies the anxiety from it. Now that we know English proficiency, instructor's personality and academic level all play some influential roles in one's activity level, let's figure out What's next.
